Recently, the probability that an asteroid could hit the Earth was updated. The planet has a diameter of about 100m, and according to the latest observations, the likelihood of the meteorite directly impacting the Earth has risen from 4.9 per cent to 5.5 per cent, which scientists say is relatively small and suggests that there is no need for undue concern. The meteorite was initially predicted to have a very small probability of approaching the Earth in 2038, but with the improvement of the observation data, the research team further confirmed that it is more likely to deviate from the Earth's orbit. The China National Space Administration (CNSA) will continue to monitor its movements, and there is no need to take additional measures at this time. Experts from the Astronomical Bureau called on the public to look at scientific predictions rationally and not to panic due to misunderstandings.
